About SimWiki

SimWiki was created to address a critical challenge in healthcare education: the high cost of simulation equipment.

The Problem

Healthcare simulation is an essential component of modern medical education, allowing students and professionals to practice clinical skills in a safe, controlled environment. However, commercial simulation equipment often comes with prohibitive price tags:

  • High-fidelity manikins can cost ¬£30,000-¬£80,000
  • Task trainers typically range from ¬£500-¬£5,000 each
  • Complete simulation center setups can exceed ¬£500,000
  • Network and AV systems add tens of thousands to costs

These expenses put quality simulation training out of reach for many institutions, particularly smaller hospitals, GP surgeries, and educational facilities with limited budgets.

The Solution

SimWiki provides a comprehensive resource for finding cost-effective alternatives to expensive simulation equipment. Our approach includes:

  • DIY Solutions: Step-by-step guides for creating your own simulation equipment using readily available materials
  • Budget Alternatives: Recommendations for commercial products that offer similar functionality at a fraction of the cost
  • Creative Workarounds: Innovative techniques for achieving training objectives without specialized equipment
  • Community Knowledge: Real-world experiences and tips from simulation educators across the UK

Focus on UK Healthcare Simulation

SimWiki is specifically designed for healthcare simulation in the United Kingdom, taking into account:

  • NHS training requirements and standards
  • UK-specific suppliers and vendors
  • British healthcare protocols and procedures
  • Compliance with UK health and safety regulations
  • Currency considerations (GBP pricing)

Important Disclaimer

Safety First: While SimWiki provides cost-effective alternatives, it's crucial to ensure that any DIY or alternative equipment meets appropriate safety and training standards. Always:

  • Consult with your institution's health and safety officers
  • Ensure compliance with relevant training accreditation requirements
  • Test all equipment thoroughly before use with learners
  • Consider whether cost savings compromise training quality
  • Document and validate your alternatives appropriately

Contributing
