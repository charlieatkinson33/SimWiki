# Consumables & Supplies

Consumable materials for simulation can add up quickly. These alternatives can save significant costs over time.

## Fake Blood

### Commercial Options
- **Mehron Stage Blood**: £8-£12 per 250ml
- **Pint O' Blood**: £15-£20 per 500ml
- Features: Non-staining, realistic color, appropriate viscosity

### DIY Recipes

#### Basic Fake Blood (£2 per liter)
**Ingredients:**
- Golden syrup or corn syrup - £1.50
- Red food coloring - £1
- Water - free
- Optional: Blue/green food coloring for darkening

**Recipe:**
1. Mix 500ml golden syrup with 200ml water
2. Add red food coloring until desired color
3. Add 1-2 drops blue for realistic darkness
4. Adjust consistency with water

**Variations:**
- Arterial blood: Brighter red, thinner consistency
- Venous blood: Darker red, slightly thicker
- Old blood: Add brown food coloring

#### Non-Staining Formula (£5 per liter)
**Ingredients:**
- Clear hair gel - £2
- Washable red poster paint - £2
- Water - free
- Red food coloring - £1

**Benefits:**
- Washes out easily
- Safe for fabrics
- Non-toxic

#### Washable Formula for Manikins (£3 per liter)
**Ingredients:**
- PVA glue diluted - £1.50
- Red food coloring - £1
- Water - free
- Glycerin for shine - £0.50

## Wound Moulage

### Commercial Options
- **3D Silicone Wounds**: £15-£50 each
- **Casualty Simulation Kit**: £200-£500
- Features: Reusable, realistic texture, various injury types

### DIY Alternatives

#### Gelatin-Based Wounds (£2-£5 per wound)
**Materials:**
- Unflavored gelatin - £3
- Glycerin - £2
- Foundation makeup (flesh tone) - £5
- Food coloring - £1
- Tissue paper or cotton - £1

**Method:**
1. Mix gelatin with hot water
2. Add glycerin for flexibility
3. Color with foundation
4. Pour into shape/mold
5. Add details with tissue and makeup

**Use Cases:**
- Burns
- Lacerations
- Abrasions
- Temporary application

#### Latex-Based Wounds (£10-£20, reusable)
**Materials:**
- Liquid latex - £8
- Tissue layers - £1
- Makeup and food coloring - £5
- Fixative - £3

**Method:**
1. Apply latex in layers with tissue
2. Build up wound edges
3. Paint and detail
4. Seal for durability

**Types:**
- Bullet wounds
- Surgical incisions
- Deep lacerations
- Chemical burns

#### Wax-Based Wounds (£15-£30 for kit)
**Materials:**
- Modeling wax or nose/scar wax - £10
- Spirit gum adhesive - £5
- Makeup palette - £10
- Sealing powder - £3

**Advantages:**
- Highly moldable
- Realistic texture
- Professional appearance
- Can be removed and reformed

## Simulated Medications

### Commercial Options
- **Sim-Use Medication Labels**: £50-£100 per set
- **Fake Drug Ampoules**: £2-£5 each
- Features: Realistic labels, safe contents

### DIY Alternatives

#### Medication Labels (Free-£10)
**Method:**
1. Create templates in Word/Canva
2. Print on label paper - £10 per pack
3. Apply to empty containers

**Sources:**
- Download templates online
- Modify from genuine packaging
- Use label maker software

**Safety:**
- Mark clearly "FOR TRAINING ONLY"
- Use bright colors for visibility
- Never use real drug names on real substances

#### Ampoule Alternatives (£0.10-£0.50 each)
**Options:**
- Small glass bottles from craft stores
- Sample perfume vials
- Laboratory supply glass vials
- Food coloring in water for content

**Filling:**
- Colored water (most drugs)
- Oil-based solutions (lipid formulations)
- Saline solution

## Bodily Fluids

### Urine Simulation

#### Recipe 1: Basic (£1 per liter)
- Water
- Yellow food coloring
- Optional: Apple juice for color/smell

#### Recipe 2: Realistic (£2 per liter)
- Water
- Yellow + green food coloring (slight tint)
- Salt (specific gravity)
- Ammonia (for smell, careful with quantity)

### Vomit Simulation

#### Basic Recipe (£3 per batch)
**Ingredients:**
- Oatmeal or cornflakes - £1
- Vegetable soup - £1
- Food coloring (yellow, green, brown) - £1
- Water

**Variations:**
- Coffee grounds for GI bleed
- Add peas/corn for chunks
- Vinegar for acid smell

### Other Fluids

#### Cerebral Spinal Fluid (CSF)
- Clear water
- Slight yellow tint (very dilute)
- No particles

#### Pleural Fluid
- Water with yellow tint
- Can add red for hemothorax
- Glycerin for viscosity

## Expired Clinical Supplies

- Hospital surplus/expired stock programs
- Medical surplus suppliers
- NHS donation schemes
- University medical schools

### What to Request
- IV bags and tubing

- Never use expired medications
- Ensure needles are safely disposed
- Clean items before use
- Store properly

## Tips for Success

### Making Realistic Blood
- Test on small samples first
- Adjust for lighting conditions
- Consider cleanup requirements
- Store in labeled containers
- Date all mixtures

### Moulage Application
- Practice on volunteers
- Take before/after photos
- Document techniques
- Build a reference library
- Allow proper drying time

### Bulk Preparation
- Make larger batches
- Store in portions
- Label clearly with dates
- Track expiration
- Share recipes with team

### Safety First
- Use food-safe ingredients where possible
- Test for allergies
- Proper ventilation
- Safe disposal methods
- COSHH assessments
