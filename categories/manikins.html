Part-Task Trainers vs Full Manikins

Consider whether you need a full manikin or if part-task trainers would suffice:

When to Use Part-Task Trainers:

  • Teaching specific procedures (venipuncture, catheterization, etc.)
  • Limited budget with focused training needs
  • High-volume repetitive practice required
  • Space-constrained training environments

When to Invest in Full Manikins:

  • Team-based scenario training
  • Comprehensive patient assessment practice
  • Multi-disciplinary training sessions
  • Accreditation requirements mandate full simulation

Maintenance & Longevity

Extending the life of budget manikins:

  • Cleaning: Use appropriate disinfectants (70% alcohol wipes)
  • Storage: Keep in cool, dry place away from direct sunlight
  • Repair: Many budget manikins can be repaired with basic materials
  • Replacement Parts: Face shields, lungs, and skins are often available separately
  • Documentation: Log usage to track wear and schedule maintenance
