# Manikins & Simulators

Full-body manikins are often the most expensive investment for simulation centers. While high-fidelity manikins have their place, many educational objectives can be achieved with modified or DIY alternatives.

## Understanding Your Needs

### High-Fidelity vs. Task-Specific
- **High-fidelity**: Physiological responses, controlled remotely (£50,000+)
- **Task-specific**: Focused on particular skills (£500-£5,000)
- **DIY alternatives**: Basic but effective for many scenarios (£100-£1,000)

### Educational Fidelity
Remember: **Psychological fidelity > Physical fidelity**
- Focus on learning objectives
- Suspension of disbelief matters most
- Cheaper can still be effective

## Manikin Maintenance and Repair

### Common Issues and Fixes

**Skin Tears:**
- Vinyl repair patches - £5
- Flexible glue - £8
- Color-matched paint - £5

**Cleaning:**
- Mild soap and water
- Alcohol wipes for surfaces
- Bleach solution (1:10) for disinfection
- Dry thoroughly

**Storage:**
- Climate controlled
- Covered when not in use
- Laid flat or properly supported
- Away from direct sunlight

### Extending Lifespan
- Gentle handling training
- Proper storage
- Regular inspection
- Preventive maintenance
- Documentation of issues

## Standardized Patients as Alternative

### When to Use Real People
- Complex communication scenarios
- History taking
- Physical examination
- Breaking bad news
- Ethical discussions

### Cost Comparison
- **Manikin:** High upfront, low ongoing
- **SPs:** Low upfront, moderate ongoing
- **Hybrid:** Best of both worlds

### SP Programs on Budget
- Medical students as SPs (experience credit)
- Drama students (portfolio building)
- Retired healthcare professionals (volunteering)
- Patient support groups (education)

## Manikin Scenarios Without High-Fidelity

### Creative Approaches

1. **Embedded Actor:**
   - Voice from hidden person
   - Real-time responses
   - Authentic interaction

2. **Pre-recorded Responses:**

   - Branching scenarios
   - Realistic dialogue

3. **Facilitator Narration:**
   - "The patient says..."

4. **Visual Cues:**
   - Cards with vital signs
   - Posted information
   - Projected data

## Safety Considerations

### Electrical Safety
- Low voltage systems only
- Proper insulation
- No water exposure
- Regular testing

### Mechanical Safety
- Smooth edges
- Secure attachments
- Weight limits
- Proper support

### Infection Control
- Cleanable surfaces
- Proper disinfection
- Single-use components where needed
- Clear cleaning protocols
