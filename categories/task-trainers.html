DIY Instructions: Foam Suture Pad

  1. Cut high-density foam to 15cm x 15cm x 5cm
  2. Cut a thin layer of softer foam for the top layer
  3. Stretch latex sheet or thick balloon rubber over the top
  4. Secure with staples or strong tape on the underside
  5. Optional: Add felt backing for stability

DIY Instructions: Basic Chest Drain Trainer

  1. Create rib structure using PVC pipe or flexible tubing arranged anatomically
  2. Layer foam sheets of varying density to simulate tissue layers
  3. Cover with foam sheet and latex to create realistic skin
  4. Include inner cavity with sealable bag for fluid
  5. Mount on wooden base for stability

Key Considerations

  • Durability: Budget alternatives typically have shorter lifespans but can be replaced multiple times for the cost of one commercial unit
  • Realism: Consider which features are essential for your training objectives
  • Maintenance: DIY models may require more frequent replacement or repair
  • Time Investment: Factor in the time needed to create DIY alternatives
  • Validation: Ensure alternatives meet your educational goals and accreditation requirements
