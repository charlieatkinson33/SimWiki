Recipe: Basic Wound Wax Alternative

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons petroleum jelly
  • 2 tablespoons flour
  • Red and purple food coloring

Instructions:

  1. Mix petroleum jelly and flour until smooth
  2. Add food coloring to desired shade
  3. Apply to skin and shape as needed
  4. Blend edges with makeup sponge

Recipe: Fake Blood

Simple Version (¬£2):

  • Golden syrup or corn syrup
  • Red food coloring
  • Small amount of blue food coloring (for depth)
  • Cocoa powder (for darker, older blood)

Realistic Version (¬£5):

  • 100ml water
  • 3 tablespoons golden syrup
  • Red food coloring (generous amount)
  • 1/2 teaspoon instant coffee granules
  • 2 drops blue food coloring
  • 1 tablespoon cornflour (for thickness)

Recipe: Gelatin Skin for Deep Wounds

Ingredients:

  • 2 packets unflavored gelatin
  • 1 tablespoon water
  • 2 tablespoons glycerin
  • Foundation makeup or food coloring

Instructions:

  1. Mix gelatin with water and heat gently
  2. Add glycerin and mix well
  3. Cool slightly and add skin-tone color
  4. Apply warm mixture to skin surface
  5. Allow to set (5-10 minutes)
  6. Cut or tear to create wound appearance

Common Wound Types & Techniques

Lacerations & Cuts (¬£1-3 per application)

  • Apply liquid latex in layers
  • Before final layer dries, create opening with toothpick
  • Add fake blood inside wound
  • Blend edges with foundation makeup

Burns (¬£2-4 per application)

  • First Degree: Red makeup with slight swelling (tissue under latex)
  • Second Degree: Blisters using gelatin bubbles, red/pink coloring
  • Third Degree: Black/brown coloring, textured with torn tissue paper

Bruising (¬£1-2 per application)

  • Layer purple, blue, yellow, and green makeup
  • Blend with sponge for gradient effect
  • Add slight swelling with tissue under makeup

Gunshot Wounds (¬£3-5 per application)

  • Create entry wound with small liquid latex crater
  • Blacken edges with makeup
  • Add blood around wound
  • Exit wound: larger opening with torn gelatin skin

Road Rash / Abrasions (¬£2-3 per application)

  • Stipple red makeup over area
  • Add texture with torn tissue paper
  • Light blood wash over surface
  • Add dirt/grime with brown makeup

Advanced DIY: Creating Reusable Prosthetics

For frequently used wounds, create reusable prosthetics:

Materials Needed (¬£50-80 initial investment):

  • Silicone molding compound (Dragon Skin or similar) - ¬£30-50
  • Plasticine clay for sculpting - ¬£5
  • Plaster of Paris for molds - ¬£5
  • Release agent - ¬£5
  • Paints and pigments - ¬£10

Basic Process:

  1. Sculpt desired wound in clay
  2. Create plaster mold around sculpture
  3. Remove clay once plaster sets
  4. Apply release agent to mold
  5. Pour silicone into mold
  6. Allow to cure (4-24 hours depending on product)
  7. Paint and detail prosthetic
  8. Attach with prosthetic adhesive

  • Black thread for visual effect

  • Embedded objects: toothpicks, craft sticks
  • Secure with liquid latex or gelatin

Application & Removal Tips

Application:

  • Test for allergies before applying to skin
  • Use barrier spray or lotion on hairy areas
  • Blend edges carefully for realism
  • Apply makeup in good lighting
  • Take photos for consistency in repeat scenarios

Removal:

  • Liquid latex: peel off gently
  • Gelatin: warm water dissolves
  • Makeup: baby oil or theatrical makeup remover
  • Stubborn adhesive: isopropyl alcohol

Storage & Hygiene

  • Store materials in cool, dry location
  • Label containers with contents and date
  • Refrigerate gelatin-based products (1 week max)
  • Use single-use applicators or clean thoroughly between uses
  • Screen actors for skin sensitivities
  • Have makeup remover and skin care products available
