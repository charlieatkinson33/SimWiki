# Contribute to SimWiki

SimWiki is a community project. Your knowledge and experience can help others create effective simulation programs on a budget.

## Why Contribute?

- **Help Others**: Share your successes with the community
- **Build Reputation**: Become known for innovation
- **Improve Education**: Make simulation more accessible
- **Learn**: Get feedback on your techniques
- **Collaborate**: Connect with other educators

## What We're Looking For

### Types of Contributions

#### Equipment Alternatives
- DIY task trainers
- Low-cost recording solutions
- Affordable props and supplies
- Technology implementations

#### Techniques and Methods
- Construction guides
- Setup procedures
- Maintenance tips
- Troubleshooting advice

#### Cost Comparisons
- Real-world pricing data
- Total cost of ownership
- Lifespan and durability
- Supplier recommendations

#### Case Studies
- Implementation stories
- Before and after comparisons
- Lessons learned
- Educational outcomes

## How to Contribute

### Option 1: GitHub (Recommended for Technical Users)

1. **Fork the Repository**
   - Visit [github.com/charlieatkinson33/SimWiki](https://github.com/charlieatkinson33/SimWiki)
   - Click "Fork" to create your copy

2. **Make Your Changes**
   - Edit existing pages or create new ones
   - Follow our style guide (below)
   - Add images to `/assets/images/`

3. **Submit a Pull Request**
   - Describe your changes
   - Include any relevant photos or diagrams
   - Wait for review and feedback

### Option 2: Email Submission

If you're not comfortable with GitHub:

1. **Write Your Content**
   - Use a Word document or plain text
   - Include all details and measurements
   - Add photos if possible

2. **Send to Us**
   - Email: [Contact through GitHub Issues]
   - Include: Your name (optional), affiliation, content

3. **We'll Format It**
   - We'll add your content to the appropriate page
   - Credit you as contributor
   - Send you the final version for approval

### Option 3: Share Your Link

Already have a blog post, video, or guide?

- Submit the link through GitHub Issues
- We'll reference it with proper attribution
- Keeps traffic to your original content

## Content Guidelines

### What to Include

#### For Equipment Designs

**Essential Information:**
- List of materials with UK suppliers
- Estimated total cost
- Construction time required
- Skill level needed
- Step-by-step instructions
- Photos of finished product
- Commercial equivalent and cost

**Helpful Additions:**
- Diagrams or blueprints
- Video demonstrations
- Common mistakes to avoid
- Variations or modifications
- Maintenance requirements
- Lifespan and durability notes

#### For Techniques

**Essential Information:**
- Problem being solved
- Materials or tools needed
- Clear procedure
- Expected outcomes
- Safety considerations

**Helpful Additions:**
- Photos or videos
- Troubleshooting guide
- Variations for different settings
- Cost analysis
- Time requirements

### Writing Style

**Be Clear and Specific:**
- Use measurements, not estimates
- Specify exact products where important
- Include model numbers when relevant
- Note any critical steps

**Be Honest:**
- Acknowledge limitations
- Note what didn't work
- Explain required compromises
- Be realistic about skill level

**Be Safe:**
- Include safety warnings
- Note hazardous materials
- Specify required precautions
- Consider user skill level

**Be Practical:**
- Focus on replicability
- Use commonly available materials
- Provide UK supplier information
- Include troubleshooting tips

### Formatting Guidelines

#### Page Structure
```markdown
---
layout: default
title: Your Title
category: category-name
---

# Main Title

## Introduction
Brief overview of the item/technique

## Commercial Options
What's available commercially and costs

## DIY Alternative
Your solution

### Materials
- Item 1 - £cost
- Item 2 - £cost

### Instructions
Step-by-step guide

### Tips and Variations
Additional helpful information

## Cost Comparison
Commercial vs DIY breakdown

## Related Categories
Links to related pages
```

#### Images
- Place in `/assets/images/category-name/`
- Use descriptive filenames
- Include alt text for accessibility
- Compress for web use (max 500KB)

#### Links
- Use relative links within SimWiki
- Use full URLs for external sites
- Check all links before submitting

## Quality Standards

### We Accept
- Working solutions, even if not perfect
- Multiple approaches to same problem
- Regional variations
- Incremental improvements to existing designs

### We Need You To Provide
- Accurate cost information
- Clear safety warnings
- Honest assessment of results
- Proper attribution of ideas

### We Don't Accept
- Unsafe practices
- Copyright infringement
- Misleading claims
- Commercial promotion

## Photography Guidelines

### What to Photograph
- Finished product from multiple angles
- Key construction steps
- Material components
- Size reference (ruler, hand)
- Product in use (if appropriate)

### Photo Quality
- Good lighting
- Clear focus
- Plain background
- High resolution (but web-optimized)
- Remove identifying information (faces, badges)

### Photo Rights
- Only submit photos you own or have permission to use
- Model releases for recognizable people
- No copyrighted materials in frame
- No patient or confidential information

## Attribution and Credit

### How We Credit Contributors
- Name and affiliation on contribution
- Link to your professional profile (optional)
- "Contributed by" note on pages
- Contributor list on About page

### What You Retain
- Copyright to your original content
- Right to use elsewhere
- Right to update your contributions
- Right to request removal

### What SimWiki Gets
- License to publish your contribution
- Right to edit for clarity/formatting
- Right to translate or adapt
- Right to maintain indefinitely

## Review Process

### What Happens After You Submit

1. **Initial Review** (1-3 days)
   - Check for completeness
   - Verify safety considerations
   - Assess quality

2. **Technical Review** (3-7 days)
   - Verify accuracy
   - Test instructions if possible
   - Check cost estimates

3. **Editing** (1-3 days)
   - Format for website
   - Add images
   - Create links

4. **Your Approval** (2-3 days)
   - Review final version
   - Request changes if needed
   - Approve for publication

5. **Publication**
   - Added to website
   - You're notified
   - Credit added

### Revision Requests
- We may ask for clarifications
- Additional photos may be requested
- Safety concerns must be addressed
- Cost estimates should be verifiable
